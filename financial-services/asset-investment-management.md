# Asset & Investment Management

_The capital-stewardship engine that researches, allocates, and manages investments to deliver risk-adjusted returns for clients and funds._

## Portfolio Manager
- Construct and rebalance portfolios in line with mandate, benchmark, and risk budget
- Make buy, sell, and hold decisions backed by research conviction
- Manage portfolio risk across sector, factor, duration, and concentration exposures
- Attribute performance and explain returns versus benchmark to stakeholders
- Set position sizing and conviction weights across the holdings
- Communicate strategy and outlook to clients, consultants, and the investment committee

## Investment Analyst / Research Analyst (buy-side)
- Generate investment recommendations with target prices and conviction levels
- Build and maintain proprietary financial models for covered names
- Conduct primary research via management meetings, channel checks, and site visits
- Pitch new ideas to portfolio managers with a clear thesis and catalysts
- Monitor holdings for thesis-breaking news, earnings, and guidance changes
- Maintain coverage of an assigned sector or theme and track competitive dynamics

## Sell-Side Equity / Credit Research Analyst
- Publish initiation, rating, and earnings-update notes for institutional clients
- Maintain forecast models and price targets or credit recommendations
- Host investor calls, conferences, and management non-deal roadshows
- Field client questions and provide timely reactions to corporate events
- Build differentiated views through proprietary surveys and data analysis
- Rank within sector coverage to grow client votes and franchise influence

## Chief Investment Officer (CIO)
- Set the overarching investment philosophy, process, and house view
- Approve strategic and tactical asset allocation across the firm's portfolios
- Chair the investment committee and arbitrate cross-team allocation decisions
- Oversee firm-wide risk appetite, leverage, and liquidity policy
- Recruit, mentor, and evaluate portfolio managers and analysts
- Represent the investment platform to key clients, boards, and regulators

## Fund Manager
- Run a specific fund product in line with its prospectus and objective
- Ensure portfolio compliance with fund mandate, limits, and liquidity rules
- Manage subscriptions, redemptions, and cash flows against investment positions
- Report fund performance, holdings, and commentary to investors and trustees
- Maintain target tracking error or active share for the fund's style
- Coordinate with operations on NAV, valuation, and fund administration

## Trader (buy-side / dealing desk)
- Execute portfolio manager orders seeking best execution and minimal market impact
- Source liquidity discreetly to avoid signalling large positions
- Select brokers, venues, and execution algorithms appropriate to each order
- Manage order timing and working strategy across the trading day
- Provide PMs with real-time market colour, liquidity, and pricing feedback
- Maintain broker relationships and oversee commission and TCA reporting

## Multi-Asset / Asset Allocation Strategist
- Design strategic and tactical asset allocation frameworks across asset classes
- Translate macro views into recommended portfolio tilts and risk positioning
- Model cross-asset correlations, scenarios, and stress tests for allocation
- Construct model portfolios and glide paths for multi-asset products
- Recommend currency, duration, and regional overlays to portfolio teams
- Monitor allocation drift and rebalancing triggers across the asset mix

## Economist / Strategist
- Produce macroeconomic forecasts for growth, inflation, rates, and policy
- Develop investment themes linking macro views to market implications
- Publish thematic and market-outlook research for internal teams and clients
- Track central bank policy, geopolitical risk, and key economic data releases
- Brief portfolio managers and the investment committee on macro scenarios
- Build econometric models and indicators to anticipate regime shifts
