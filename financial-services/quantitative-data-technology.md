# Quantitative, Data & Technology

*The group that builds the models, research, and systematic strategies turning financial data and mathematics into pricing, risk, and trading capability.*

## Quantitative Analyst (Quant)

- Implement pricing and valuation models for derivatives and structured products in production libraries.
- Compute risk sensitivities (greeks) and ensure consistency between front-office and risk valuations.
- Calibrate models to market quotes and maintain volatility surfaces and curve construction.
- Support traders with ad hoc pricing, hedging analytics, and term-sheet structuring.
- Test and document model code, ensuring numerical stability and performance.

## Quant Researcher

- Formulate and test hypotheses for alpha signals using statistical and machine-learning methods.
- Source, clean, and engineer features from market, fundamental, and alternative datasets.
- Back-test signal and strategy ideas with realistic transaction cost and slippage assumptions.
- Publish research findings and translate promising signals into candidate strategies.
- Investigate model decay and overfitting through out-of-sample and cross-validation analysis.

## Risk Modeller / Model Developer

- Develop credit, market, and capital models (PD, LGD, VaR, economic capital) for risk measurement.
- Translate regulatory methodologies (IRB, FRTB, IFRS 9) into working model implementations.
- Build the data pipelines and calibration routines that feed risk models.
- Document model methodology and assumptions to satisfy validation and audit.
- Maintain and recalibrate deployed risk models as data and regulation evolve.

## Data Scientist (finance)

- Apply predictive and classification models to problems like churn, default, and fraud detection.
- Build and evaluate feature pipelines from structured and unstructured financial data.
- Communicate model results and limitations to business stakeholders with clear visualisations.
- Deploy models into production and monitor for performance drift and data quality issues.
- Run experiments and A/B tests to quantify the business impact of model-driven decisions.

## Financial Engineer

- Design and structure bespoke financial products and payoffs to meet client or balance-sheet needs.
- Build cash-flow engines and pricing frameworks for complex and exotic instruments.
- Engineer hedging and replication strategies for structured exposures.
- Bridge quantitative theory and technology implementation in product development.
- Assess the risk, capital, and accounting treatment of engineered structures.

## Algorithmic / Systematic Strategy Developer

- Code, test, and deploy automated trading strategies and execution algorithms.
- Optimise order routing, scheduling, and microstructure-aware execution logic.
- Build low-latency, fault-tolerant infrastructure for live strategy operation.
- Monitor live strategy performance, risk limits, and kill-switch controls.
- Conduct post-trade analysis to refine execution and reduce market impact.
