# Compliance, Legal & Financial Crime

*The group that keeps the organisation within legal and regulatory bounds and defends it against money laundering, fraud, and sanctions exposure.*

## Chief Compliance Officer (CCO)

- Own the firm-wide compliance framework, policies, and the annual compliance risk assessment.
- Report compliance posture, breaches, and remediation status to the board and regulators.
- Set the regulatory training curriculum and attest to the adequacy of the control environment.
- Approve the compliance monitoring plan and allocate resources across regulatory priorities.
- Serve as the senior accountable person for conduct and regulatory relationships.

## Compliance Analyst / Officer

- Perform second-line monitoring and testing of business controls against regulatory requirements.
- Review marketing, communications, and personal account dealing for policy adherence.
- Log, investigate, and track compliance incidents and breaches to closure.
- Provide day-to-day advisory to business lines on regulatory rules and internal policy.
- Maintain compliance registers such as gifts, conflicts of interest, and outside business activities.

## Regulatory Affairs Specialist

- Track upcoming regulation and rule changes across relevant jurisdictions and assess applicability.
- Coordinate regulatory filings, returns, and licence applications within deadline.
- Draft responses to consultations and manage regulator information requests and exams.
- Translate new regulatory obligations into internal policy and control changes.
- Maintain the regulatory inventory mapping rules to owners and controls.

## AML / KYC Analyst

- Conduct customer due diligence and enhanced due diligence at onboarding and periodic review.
- Verify beneficial ownership, source of funds, and source of wealth documentation.
- Assign and refresh customer risk ratings based on geography, product, and behaviour.
- Clear KYC remediation backlogs and escalate high-risk relationships for approval.
- Maintain accurate customer records and audit trails for regulatory inspection.

## Financial Crime / Fraud Investigator

- Investigate alerts and referrals for money laundering, fraud, and other financial crime typologies.
- Reconstruct transaction flows and gather evidence to support or dismiss suspicion.
- File suspicious activity reports (SARs/STRs) with financial intelligence units.
- Recommend account restrictions, exits, or law enforcement liaison based on findings.
- Identify emerging fraud patterns and feed intelligence back into detection rules.

## Sanctions Screening Analyst

- Screen customers, counterparties, and transactions against OFAC, UN, EU, and local sanctions lists.
- Investigate and disposition screening hits, distinguishing true matches from false positives.
- Tune screening rules and fuzzy-matching thresholds to balance coverage and noise.
- Block, freeze, or reject prohibited transactions and report to relevant authorities.
- Maintain list management and ensure timely updates of watchlist data.

## Legal Counsel (financial / transactional)

- Draft and negotiate ISDAs, GMRAs, loan agreements, and other transactional documentation.
- Advise on the legal enforceability, netting, and collateral aspects of financial contracts.
- Manage external counsel on litigation, disputes, and complex transactions.
- Review structured products and new business for legal risk and documentation gaps.
- Provide legal opinions on regulatory interpretation and cross-border transaction structures.
