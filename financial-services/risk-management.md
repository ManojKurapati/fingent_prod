# Risk Management

*The group that identifies, measures, and governs financial and non-financial risk across the enterprise to keep exposure within board-approved appetite.*

## Chief Risk Officer (CRO)

- Set the enterprise risk appetite framework and present aggregate risk positions to the board and risk committee.
- Own the firm-wide risk governance structure, escalation paths, and committee mandates.
- Approve risk limits and challenge business strategy where it conflicts with the risk profile.
- Act as the senior point of contact for regulators on prudential and risk matters.
- Sponsor risk culture initiatives and ensure independence of the risk function from revenue lines.

## Market Risk Analyst / Manager

- Calculate VaR, expected shortfall, and sensitivity (greeks) measures across trading and banking books.
- Run daily P&L attribution and investigate breaches of market risk limits.
- Design and execute stress tests and scenario analyses for rate, FX, equity, and commodity shocks.
- Monitor intraday and end-of-day positions against desk and entity limits.
- Validate market data, pricing inputs, and the accuracy of risk factor mappings.

## Credit Risk Analyst / Manager

- Assess borrower and counterparty creditworthiness using financial statements, ratings, and PD/LGD models.
- Set and monitor counterparty credit limits, including potential future exposure on derivatives.
- Manage the credit approval workflow and recommend terms, covenants, and collateral requirements.
- Track portfolio concentration, migration, and default trends across obligors and sectors.
- Calculate expected credit loss (IFRS 9 / CECL) provisions and impairment staging.

## Operational Risk Manager

- Maintain the operational risk taxonomy, RCSA process, and loss-event database.
- Define and monitor key risk indicators (KRIs) for process, people, and systems failures.
- Investigate operational loss events and drive root-cause remediation with business owners.
- Assess operational risk in new products, outsourcing, and change initiatives.
- Calculate operational risk capital and report incident trends to governance forums.

## Liquidity Risk Manager

- Monitor liquidity coverage ratio (LCR), net stable funding ratio (NSFR), and survival horizons.
- Build cash-flow projections and run liquidity stress scenarios under idiosyncratic and market-wide stress.
- Oversee the contingency funding plan and intraday liquidity management.
- Set early-warning indicators and funding concentration limits across counterparties and tenors.
- Optimise the high-quality liquid asset buffer against regulatory and internal targets.

## Model Risk / Validation Analyst

- Independently validate pricing, risk, and capital models for conceptual soundness and implementation accuracy.
- Benchmark model outputs against alternative methods and back-test against realised outcomes.
- Maintain the model inventory, tiering, and validation schedule per model risk policy.
- Document model limitations and assumptions, and track remediation of validation findings.
- Review ongoing model performance monitoring and trigger revalidation when drift is detected.

## Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) Lead

- Integrate market, credit, operational, and liquidity risk into a consolidated firm-wide risk view.
- Coordinate the firm's risk appetite statement, key metrics, and aggregate dashboard reporting.
- Run the top-down enterprise stress testing and ICAAP/ILAAP processes.
- Facilitate emerging risk horizon scanning and the firm's risk register reviews.
- Align risk reporting cadence across business units and the second line of defence.
