# Internal Audit & Controls

_The independent assurance function that tests controls, evaluates risk, and protects the integrity of financial processes._

## Chief Audit Executive
- Set the risk-based internal audit plan and present it to the audit committee.
- Lead the internal audit function with independence from management.
- Report audit findings and control deficiencies to the board.
- Evaluate the adequacy of the organisation's governance and risk frameworks.
- Coordinate with external auditors and regulators on assurance matters.

## Internal Audit Manager
- Plan and scope individual audit engagements against the annual plan.
- Lead audit teams through fieldwork and testing.
- Review workpapers and draft audit reports with recommendations.
- Track remediation of prior findings to closure.
- Assess process risks and design audit programs.

## Internal Auditor
- Execute audit testing and gather supporting evidence.
- Document control walkthroughs and identify gaps.
- Prepare workpapers detailing procedures and results.
- Draft findings and proposed recommendations.
- Follow up with process owners on corrective actions.

## SOX / Internal Controls Analyst
- Maintain the SOX control matrix and process narratives.
- Test the design and operating effectiveness of key controls.
- Document control deficiencies and assess their severity.
- Coordinate management's control self-assessments.
- Support external auditors' reliance on internal control testing.
