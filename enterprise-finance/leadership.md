# Leadership

_The senior executives who set financial direction, own capital strategy, and hold ultimate accountability for the organisation's fiscal health._

## Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
- Set the overall financial strategy and align capital allocation with long-term corporate objectives.
- Present financial results, guidance, and risk posture to the board, investors, and lenders.
- Own enterprise-wide capital structure decisions including debt, equity, and dividend policy.
- Sponsor major financial transformation and AI-automation initiatives across the finance function.
- Sign off on annual budgets, long-range plans, and material financial commitments.
- Serve as the executive accountable for the integrity of external financial reporting.

## Deputy / Divisional CFO
- Run finance for a specific business unit, region, or division on behalf of the group CFO.
- Translate group-level financial targets into divisional operating plans and accountability.
- Deputise for the CFO in board sessions, investor calls, and cross-functional executive forums.
- Drive P&L ownership and margin improvement within the assigned division.
- Coordinate divisional finance teams to feed consistent data into group consolidation.

## VP of Finance
- Oversee the day-to-day operation of the finance organisation across FP&A, accounting, and reporting.
- Convert CFO strategy into executable departmental roadmaps, budgets, and headcount plans.
- Lead month-end, quarter-end, and year-end close governance and timeliness.
- Manage relationships with external auditors, banks, and key financial vendors.
- Build and develop the finance leadership bench through hiring and succession planning.

## Finance Director
- Direct a defined finance domain (e.g. reporting, operations, or a region) toward measurable targets.
- Approve financial policies, controls, and process standards within their remit.
- Lead the preparation and review of management accounts and variance commentary.
- Partner with operational leaders to embed financial discipline into business decisions.
- Escalate emerging financial risks and resourcing gaps to executive leadership.

## Head of Finance
- Manage the core finance team and own delivery of accurate, on-time financial outputs.
- Coordinate budgeting, forecasting, and reporting cycles for the assigned entity or function.
- Maintain financial controls and ensure compliance with internal policies and standards.
- Act as the primary finance point of contact for operational department heads.
- Review and approve routine financial transactions, reconciliations, and journal postings.
