# Financial Planning & Analysis (FP&A)

_The forward-looking analytical engine that plans, forecasts, and translates financial data into decisions that steer the business._

## Head of FP&A
- Own the enterprise planning calendar covering annual budgets and long-range strategic plans.
- Define the FP&A operating model, modelling standards, and planning toolset.
- Present consolidated forecasts and scenario analyses to executive leadership.
- Lead the FP&A team and set analytical priorities across business partners.
- Govern the integrity of planning assumptions and driver-based models.

## FP&A Manager
- Coordinate the recurring forecast and budget cycles across contributing teams.
- Review analyst outputs for accuracy before they reach leadership.
- Maintain consolidated planning models and reporting packages.
- Manage variance reviews and chase down explanations from cost-centre owners.
- Improve planning processes and reduce cycle time through automation.

## Financial Analyst
- Build and update financial models supporting budgets and forecasts.
- Prepare monthly variance analyses comparing actuals to plan.
- Gather and validate input data from across the business.
- Produce recurring management reports and dashboards.
- Support ad hoc analysis requests from finance and operational teams.

## Senior Financial Analyst
- Lead complex modelling efforts such as multi-year scenario and sensitivity analysis.
- Mentor junior analysts and review their deliverables for quality.
- Develop new analytical frameworks and reporting templates.
- Drive deep-dive investigations into margin, cost, or revenue anomalies.
- Partner directly with mid-level business leaders on financial questions.

## Budgeting & Forecasting Analyst
- Compile and reconcile departmental budget submissions into a consolidated plan.
- Maintain rolling forecasts and update them as actuals and assumptions change.
- Track budget-versus-actual performance and flag overspend risks early.
- Administer the planning system's hierarchies, accounts, and allocation rules.
- Document budget assumptions and changes for audit and review.

## Business / Commercial Finance Partner
- Embed with a business unit to advise on commercial and operational decisions.
- Model pricing, deal economics, and investment cases for the supported team.
- Translate operational drivers into financial outcomes and targets.
- Challenge business assumptions and surface profitability trade-offs.
- Bridge the business unit's needs back into the central planning process.

## Decision Support / Strategic Finance Analyst
- Build business cases for new initiatives, products, and market entries.
- Run strategic scenario modelling to inform executive choices.
- Quantify the financial impact of strategic options and trade-offs.
- Synthesise market, competitor, and internal data into decision-ready insight.
- Support special projects and one-off strategic analyses for leadership.

## Revenue Analyst
- Forecast revenue by product, channel, segment, and geography.
- Analyse bookings, billings, churn, and renewal trends.
- Reconcile reported revenue against operational pipeline and CRM data.
- Model the revenue impact of pricing and packaging changes.
- Report on key revenue KPIs such as ARR, retention, and growth rates.
