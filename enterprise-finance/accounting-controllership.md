# Accounting & Controllership

_The custodians of the ledger who ensure every transaction is recorded accurately, compliantly, and in line with accounting standards._

## Corporate Controller
- Own the integrity of the general ledger and the consolidated financial statements.
- Govern the close calendar and ensure timely, accurate period-end results.
- Establish accounting policies and ensure compliance with GAAP or IFRS.
- Manage the external audit and resolve technical accounting questions.
- Lead the accounting organisation and its control environment.

## Assistant Controller
- Supervise day-to-day accounting operations and close execution.
- Review reconciliations, journal entries, and supporting schedules.
- Deputise for the Controller on reporting and audit matters.
- Coordinate sub-ledger teams to deliver clean, on-time inputs.
- Drive process and control improvements across the accounting cycle.

## Chief Accounting Officer (CAO)
- Set enterprise accounting policy and technical accounting positions.
- Sign off on the accuracy of statutory and regulatory filings.
- Represent accounting in executive and audit-committee discussions.
- Oversee global accounting standards adoption and change management.
- Hold ultimate accountability for the books and records of the company.

## Accounting Manager
- Manage a team of accountants through the monthly close.
- Assign and review journal entries, accruals, and reconciliations.
- Ensure sub-ledgers tie out to the general ledger each period.
- Resolve accounting discrepancies and exceptions.
- Maintain documentation supporting balances and processes.

## General Ledger Accountant
- Post and review journal entries across the chart of accounts.
- Reconcile GL accounts and clear reconciling items.
- Prepare period-end accruals, prepayments, and adjusting entries.
- Maintain the chart of accounts and cost-centre structures.
- Support audit requests with GL detail and supporting evidence.

## Staff / Senior Accountant
- Prepare and post routine journal entries and accruals.
- Perform monthly balance-sheet account reconciliations.
- Compile supporting schedules for close and audit.
- Investigate and resolve transactional discrepancies.
- Assist with documentation and process notes for the close.

## Cost Accountant
- Calculate and maintain standard costs for products and services.
- Analyse manufacturing or service cost variances against standard.
- Value inventory and reconcile inventory sub-ledgers.
- Allocate overhead and indirect costs to cost objects.
- Report on margins and cost drivers to operations teams.

## Fixed Assets Accountant
- Maintain the fixed-asset register and capitalisation records.
- Calculate and post depreciation and amortisation schedules.
- Account for asset additions, disposals, and transfers.
- Reconcile the fixed-asset sub-ledger to the general ledger.
- Coordinate physical asset verification and impairment reviews.

## Technical Accounting / Revenue Recognition Specialist
- Interpret complex standards such as ASC 606 / IFRS 15 for new contracts.
- Draft technical accounting memos to support judgemental positions.
- Review customer contracts for revenue-recognition implications.
- Advise on accounting for non-routine and structured transactions.
- Liaise with auditors on technical conclusions and disclosures.

## Consolidations Accountant
- Consolidate financial results across legal entities and currencies.
- Process and eliminate intercompany transactions and balances.
- Apply foreign-currency translation and remeasurement.
- Reconcile consolidated outputs to entity-level submissions.
- Prepare consolidation journals and group reporting packages.
